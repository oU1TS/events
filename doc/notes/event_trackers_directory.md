## Guide: How to Strategically Utilize This Directory

Having a massive list of event trackers is only useful if you know how to filter the noise. Use this step-by-step approach to maximize your efficiency, portfolio growth, and networking potential.

### Step 1: Align Trackers to Your Current Career Goal

Do not try to monitor every tier at once. Filter your tracking based on what you need *right now*:

* **For Job Hunting & Resume Building:** Focus heavily on the **Primary Tier** (Devpost, HackerEarth, Unstop). Corporate-sponsored hackathons on these platforms are frequently used as direct hiring funnels, bypassing cold resume submissions.


* **For Technical Interview Prep:** Stick to the **Competitive Programming Tier** (LeetCode, Codeforces). Use their weekly tracked schedules to build consistency under timed pressure.


* **For AI/ML Domain Authority:** Monitor the **Specialty Tier** (Kaggle, AICrowd). Winning or placing highly on these trackers carries massive weight with AI enterprise recruiters and research institutions.


* **For Local Networking & Freelancing:** Prioritize the **Industry Tier** (Luma, Meetup) to find regional hacker houses, demo days, and tech mixers.



### Step 2: Establish an Optimization Workflow

To prevent "tab fatigue" (opening 20 websites every week), set up a systematic monitoring routine:

1. **Set a Bi-Weekly Calendar Block:** Dedicate 30 minutes every other Sunday to scan your top 3 chosen trackers.


2. **Filter by "Registration Open" and "Prize Pool":** Most major hackathon trackers (like Devpost or MLH) allow you to filter out events that haven't opened yet or lack significant backing.


3. **Track the "CFP" (Call for Papers) Timelines:** If you want to build a brand as a tech speaker, use `Developers.events` or `Confs.tech` specifically to track deadlines for speaker submissions 3–6 months in advance.



### Step 3: Leverage Game Jams for Rapid Prototyping

If you are struggling with a creative block or need to learn a new framework quickly, use the **Creative Tier** (Itch.io, Ludum Dare).

* Game jams force you to build and ship a complete, working product within 48 to 72 hours.


* Even if you aren't a traditional game dev, the strict time constraints are excellent for practicing project management, scoping, and rapid deployment.



### Step 4: Keep a Master "Build Log"

Every time you enter an event discovered through these trackers, ensure it feeds back into your portfolio:

* Document your code repositories on GitHub.


* Link your project submission page from Devpost or Lablab.ai back to your personal portfolio site.


* Update your **Codolio Event Tracker** profile to cleanly display your contest history and competitive programming milestones automatically.
